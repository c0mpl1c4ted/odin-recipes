Pho

Description

Pho is Vietnam’s national dish — a fragrant, savory broth served with rice noodles, herbs, and thinly sliced beef. This recipe walks you through creating an authentic beef pho from scratch, capturing the balance of spices, beefy richness, and fresh garnishes that make pho so beloved.

Ingredients

For the broth:
  • 2.5–3 pounds beef bones (knuckle, marrow, or leg bones)
  • 1 pound beef brisket or chuck
  • 1 large onion, halved
  • 1 large piece of ginger (about 4 inches), halved lengthwise
  • 1 cinnamon stick
  • 4 star anise pods
  • 4 cloves
  • 2 cardamom pods (optional)
  • 1 tablespoon coriander seeds
  • 1 tablespoon salt (to taste)
  • 1 tablespoon sugar (rock sugar if available)
  • 2 tablespoons fish sauce (or more to taste)
  • 10 cups water
For the bowl:
  • 1 pound rice noodles (bánh phở), medium-width
  • Half a pound eye of round steak or sirloin, thinly sliced (freeze for 15 minutes to make slicing easier)
  • Fresh herbs: Thai basil, cilantro, and mint
  • Bean sprouts
  • Lime wedges
  • Jalapeños or Thai chilies, sliced
  • Thinly sliced onion
  • Hoisin sauce and sriracha (optional)

Steps

  1. Place onion and ginger directly over a gas flame or under a broiler. Char until blackened, about 5–8 minutes, turning occasionally.
  2. Rinse to remove loose char and set aside.
  3. In a large pot, cover beef bones with cold water. Bring to a boil and let it boil for 10 minutes.
  4. Drain and rinse the bones under cool water to remove impurities.
  5. Clean the pot or use a fresh one, then add the bones back in.
  6. Add 10 cups of fresh water to the pot with the bones.
  7. Add charred onion, ginger, brisket, and all spices.
  8. Bring to a boil, then reduce to a gentle simmer.
  9. Skim off any scum that rises to the top.
  10. Simmer uncovered for at least 4–5 hours (the longer, the better; 6–8 hours is ideal). Remove brisket after 1.5–2 hours, once tender, and set aside.
  11. After simmering, strain the broth into another pot to remove solids.
  12. Add fish sauce, sugar, and salt to taste.
  13. Slice the cooked brisket thinly against the grain.
  14. Soak rice noodles in warm water for 30–45 minutes or according to package instructions.
  15. Boil for 30–60 seconds until soft but not mushy. Drain and rinse with cool water.
  16. Divide noodles into bowls.
  17. Top with slices of raw beef and brisket.
  18. Ladle hot broth directly over meat to cook it (it should be boiling hot).
  19. Garnish with herbs, onions, and optional sauces.
  20. Serve with lime, chilies, and bean sprouts on the side.
