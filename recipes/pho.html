Pho

Description

Pho is Vietnam’s national dish — a fragrant, savory broth served with rice noodles, herbs, and thinly sliced beef. This recipe walks you through creating an authentic beef pho from scratch, capturing the balance of spices, beefy richness, and fresh garnishes that make pho so beloved.

Ingredients

For the broth:
  • 2.5–3 pounds beef bones (knuckle, marrow, or leg bones)
  • 1 pound beef brisket or chuck
  • 1 large onion, halved
  • 1 large piece of ginger (about 4 inches), halved lengthwise
  • 1 cinnamon stick
  • 4 star anise pods
  • 4 cloves
  • 2 cardamom pods (optional)
  • 1 tablespoon coriander seeds
  • 1 tablespoon salt (to taste)
  • 1 tablespoon sugar (rock sugar if available)
  • 2 tablespoons fish sauce (or more to taste)
  • 10 cups water
For the bowl:
  • 1 pound rice noodles (bánh phở), medium-width
  • Half a pound eye of round steak or sirloin, thinly sliced (freeze for 15 minutes to make slicing easier)
  • Fresh herbs: Thai basil, cilantro, and mint
  • Bean sprouts
  • Lime wedges
  • Jalapeños or Thai chilies, sliced
  • Thinly sliced onion
  • Hoisin sauce and sriracha (optional)

Steps

  1. Prepare the Bones & Meat:
    • Parboil the bones: Place bones and optional brisket in a large pot, cover with water, and bring to a boil for 10 minutes to release impurities.
    • Drain and rinse bones/meat under cool water. Scrub the pot clean to ensure a clear broth.
  2. Roast the Aromatics:
    • Char the onion and ginger: Place them under a broiler or directly over a gas flame until slightly blackened (about 5–7 minutes). This adds depth and aroma.
  3. Toast the Spices:
    • In a dry pan, toast star anise, cloves, cinnamon stick, and coriander seeds over medium heat until fragrant (1–2 minutes). Be careful not to burn them.
  4. Simmer the Broth:
    • Return bones and brisket (if using) to the clean pot.
    • Add 4–5 quarts of water, charred onion and ginger, toasted spices, fish sauce, sugar, and a pinch of salt.
    • Bring to a boil, then reduce to a low simmer. Skim off any foam or scum that rises.
    • Simmer uncovered for at least 3 hours (or up to 6 for deeper flavor).
    • Remove the brisket after 1.5–2 hours and slice thinly.
    • Strain the broth through a fine mesh sieve, discard solids. Taste and adjust with more fish sauce, salt, or sugar.
  5. Prepare the Noodles
    • Soak dried rice noodles in warm water for 30 minutes, then briefly boil for 30–60 seconds until tender. Drain and rinse with cold water.
  6. Assemble the Pho:
    • In each bowl, place a portion of noodles.
    • Add slices of cooked brisket (if using) and raw beef slices.
    • Pour hot broth directly over the raw beef to cook it instantly.
    • Top with sliced onions and herbs.
  7. Garnish and Serve:
    • Serve with a plate of fresh herbs, lime wedges, bean sprouts, and chilies.
    • Add hoisin and Sriracha to taste.
